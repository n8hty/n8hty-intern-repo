# Introduction to UX Research Methods

## If Focus Bear wanted to improve its onboarding experience, which UX research methods would be most useful?
For Focus Bear, usability testing and user interviews would be very useful because they show where new users get confused or overwhelmed during onboarding. 
Surveys and analytics could also help identify where users stop or skip steps in the setup process.

## What are the "jobs to be done" for Focus Bear users? (include the different categories of jobs not just the obvious ones)
The obvious job is helping users stay focused and productive, but there are also emotional and behavioral jobs. 
Users may want to reduce distractions, build routines, feel less overwhelmed, manage ADHD-related struggles, or feel more in control of their time and habits.

## What challenges might arise when collecting self-reported user feedback?
Users may forget details, give unclear answers, or say what they think researchers want to hear instead of their actual experience. 
Some users may also struggle to explain why they feel frustrated or distracted while using the app.

## How can UX designers avoid bias when conducting research?
UX designers can avoid bias by asking neutral questions, listening carefully, and testing with different types of users. 
It is also important not to assume problems before collecting real feedback and data.

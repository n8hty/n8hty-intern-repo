# Agile Workflows & Kanban

## 1. How does a Kanban board work, and how does it help manage workflow?
The Kanban board works as a system used to visualize workflows, manage tasks and priorities, and overall improve team collaboration.
Tasks are written inside a card, which is moved across the board to different columns that represents its current status.

It manages workflows by:
- allowing teams to see what tasks needs to be completed
- improving priorities management
- tracking project/team progress

## 2. What do the different columns on a Kanban board represent?
What the columns contain depends on the project the team is working on. 

Most common columns are the ff:
- ### Backlog
Tasks that have not been started yet.

- ### To-Do
Tasks that are ready to be worked on next.

- ### In Progress
Tasks that are currently being worked on.

- ### Ready for Review
Tasks that are up for team review.

- ### Blocked
Tasks that cannot continue due to issues.

- ### Done
Tasks that are reviewed and completed.

## 3. How do tasks move through the board, and who is responsible for updating them?
Tasks are moved through the board when it progresses from one status to another by the person assigned to that particular task.

## 4. What are the benefits of limiting work in progress (WIP)?
The benefits of limiting WIP helps the team:
- focus on the high-priority tasks
- reduce overload and avoid burnout
- improve team efficiency and productivity

# Reflection

## 1. How does Kanban help manage priorities and avoid overload?
The Kanban helps manage priorities and avoid overload because it clearly shows which tasks are most important. Fewer tasks on the to-do list avoids the feeling of being overwhelmed.

## 2. How can you improve your workflow using Kanban principles?
Using Kanban principles helps me stay organized despite having numerous tasks on the list and allows me to focus on one task at a time.

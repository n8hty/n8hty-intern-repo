# Agile Workflows & Kanban

## How does a Kanban board work, and how does it help manage workflow?
A Kanban board is a visual system used to track tasks as they move through different stages of work. 
Tasks are represented as cards and organized into columns that show the current status of the work.

Kanban helps manage workflow by:
- improving visibility of tasks
- helping teams track progress
- improving organization and collaboration

## What do the different columns on a Kanban board represent?

### 1. Backlog
Tasks that have not been started yet.

### 2. To-Do
Tasks that are ready to be worked on next.

### 3. In Progress
Tasks that are currently being worked on.

### 4. Blocked
Tasks that cannot continue because of an issue or dependency.

### 5. Done
Tasks that have been completed.

## How do tasks move through the board, and who is responsible for updating them?
Tasks move across the board as work progresses from one stage to another. Team members responsible for the task usually update the task status to reflect current progress.

## What are the benefits of limiting work in progress (WIP)?
Limiting work in progress helps teams:
- focus on fewer tasks at a time
- reduce overload and multitasking
- improve productivity
- complete tasks more efficiently

# Reflection

## How does Kanban help manage priorities and avoid overload?
Kanban helps manage priorities by clearly showing which tasks are most important and what stage they are in. 
It avoids overload by limiting the number of tasks being worked on at the same time.

## How can you improve your workflow using Kanban principles?
I can improve my workflow by organizing tasks clearly, updating statuses regularly, focusing on one task at a time, and tracking progress consistently.

# Design Principles at Focus Bear

## What are Focus Bear’s design priorities? (Simplicity, low-friction interactions, accessibility)
Priorities should focus on being simple enough to avoid cognitive load, low-frictions to avoid confusion, and good accessibility for better engagement.
This helps users have better app retention.

## How should UI elements, colors, and typography support neurodivergent users?
UI elements, colors, and typography should help stimulate users into engaging and not being overwhelmed and intimidated.

## Why does consistency in navigation & interaction patterns matter for usability?
Consistency in navigation & interaction patterns matter for usability because it builds familiarity, especially for AuDHD users who are susceptible to losing focus quickly.

## How can small UI details (e.g., button placement, animations, tooltips) improve or harm usability?
Small UI details can improve usability by making features easier to understand and use. However, too many animations, poor button placement, or distracting tooltips can confuse and overwhelm users.

# Reflection

## How can Focus Bear maintain a minimal, distraction-free UI while still offering powerful features?
Focus Bear can keep a minimal and distraction-free UI by showing only the most important features first and hiding advanced options until needed. 
Clear navigation, simple layouts, and consistent design can make the app feel easy to use without removing functionality.

## If a design change improves aesthetics but reduces usability, how should UX handle it?
UX designers should prioritize usability first. A design should look good, but it also needs to be simple, clear, and easy for users to use.

## What are the risks of overloading users with too many choices or settings?
Too many choices can overwhelm users, increase confusion, slow decision-making, and make the app harder to use for neurodivergent users.

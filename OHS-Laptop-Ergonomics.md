# Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) for Desk-Based Work

## What equipment changes can you make to improve your workspace setup? (e.g., using an external monitor, adjusting your chair, using a laptop stand)
- Use a laptop stand to raise the screen to eye level.
- Connect an external keyboard and mouse to maintain a comfortable arm position.
- Adjust my chair height so that my feet rest flat on the floor.
- Use an external monitor for a larger and more ergonomic viewing area.
- Keep frequently used items within easy reach to avoid excessive stretching.
- Ensure proper lighting to reduce eye strain.

## What behavioural changes can you implement to improve posture and reduce strain? (e.g., sitting upright, taking regular breaks, adjusting screen height)
- Sit upright with my back supported by the chair.
- Keep my shoulders relaxed and wrists in a neutral position.
- Position the screen at eye level to avoid bending my neck.
- Follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.
- Take short movement and stretching breaks every hour.
- Avoid staying in the same position for long periods.

## How can you remind yourself to maintain good posture and take breaks throughout the day? (Hint: Use Focus Bear to schedule movement breaks!)
- Use Focus Bear to schedule movement and stretching breaks throughout the day.
- Set hourly reminders to stand up, stretch, and walk around for a few minutes.
- Create a routine that includes posture checks every 30–60 minutes.
- Enable notifications or alarms that remind me to relax my shoulders and sit upright.
- Pair breaks with regular activities, such as drinking water or checking messages, to make them easier to remember.

# Working with PMs to Prioritize UX Work

## How do PMs decide which UX improvements to prioritize?
PMs usually prioritize UX improvements based on user impact, business goals, deadlines, and development effort. 
Problems that affect many users or strongly impact the product experience are often prioritized first.

## What factors influence prioritization? (e.g., user impact, development effort, business goals)
Prioritization depends on factors like user frustration, technical difficulty, business value, and available time. 
Teams also consider usability testing results and development resources before deciding what to work on first.

## How can UX designers make a strong case for usability improvements?
UX designers can use user feedback, usability testing, and examples of user confusion to support their ideas. 
Showing how a usability issue affects engagement, productivity, or retention makes the improvement more convincing.

## What frameworks help balance UX work with feature development? (e.g., RICE, MoSCoW)
Frameworks like RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) and MoSCoW (Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, Won’t-have) help teams decide which tasks are most important. 
These frameworks help balance user needs, business goals, and development timelines.

# Reflection

## If a PM tells you there’s no time for a UX improvement, how would you respond?
If a PM says there’s no time, I would explain the user impact and suggest a smaller improvement that can still help users. 
For example, I might focus only on fixing the most confusing screen instead of redesigning the whole flow.

## How can UX designers ensure that important usability issues don’t get ignored?
UX designers can document issues clearly and support them with user feedback or usability testing results. 
Regular communication with PMs and developers also helps keep these concerns visible during development.

## What’s the best way to present UX work as a business priority rather than a "nice-to-have"?
UX work should be connected to business results like better retention, fewer user errors, and higher engagement. 
Improving usability can help users stay productive and continue using the app consistently.

# Working in an Agile Team

## What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?

### Agile Project Management
It is an iterative approach with a focus on flexibility and rapid delivery of working products. Basically, it builds the project, test, then adds features gradually.

### Traditional Project Management
It is also known as the waterfall method, symbolizing a linear, sequential approach where prjects progress through different phases. Basically, it plans and develops 
everything before testing, then launching once complete.

## What are the core values and principles of Agile? (Hint: Check the Agile Manifesto.)

### 4 Core Values:
- ### Individuals and Interactions over Processes and Tools
- ### Working Software over Comprehensive Documentation
- ### Customer Collaboration over Contract Negotiation
- ### Responding to Change over Following a Plan

### Main Principles of Agile:
- ### Deliver useful work frequently
- ### Collaborate closely with customers
- ### Communicate regularly and directly
- ### Focus on simplicity
- ### Continuously improve through feedback and reflection

## What are the differences between Scrum and Kanban, and when is each used?

### Scrum 
It is used when work can be divided into milestones, and when iterations are important.
- fixed sprints
- not flexible to changes
- regular meetings

### Kanban
It is used when priorities in a project changes frequently, and continuously completes tasks.
- continuous workflow
- flexible to changes
- meetings are not required

## Why does Focus Bear lean towards Kanban instead of Scrum?
Focus Bear likely leans more toward Kanban, because it is still a relatively young app and it is still continuously improving and evolving its systems and features.
In this case, priorities and tasks change frequently based on user feedback and ongoing development needs. This makes it easier for the team to adapt to changes
quickly and efficiently without being restricted by fixed sprints.

# How to Give and Receive Design Critique

## What is design critique, and why is it a crucial part of the UX process?
Design critique is reviewing a design and giving feedback to improve it. It helps designers spot usability problems, get different perspectives, and make better decisions for users.

## What are the key principles of giving helpful, constructive feedback?
Good feedback is clear, respectful, and specific. It should focus on improving the design instead of criticizing the designer.

## How should designers receive and apply critique without taking it personally?
Designers should stay open-minded, listen carefully, and use feedback as a chance to improve instead of taking it personally.

## What are common mistakes when giving or receiving design feedback?
Some mistakes are giving vague comments, focusing only on personal preferences, being too defensive, or ignoring the users’ or company needs.

# Reflection

## What is the difference between personal opinion and objective UX critique?
Personal opinion is based on personal preference, while UX critique is based on usability and how well the design works for the target users.

## If someone gives vague feedback like "I don’t like this", how would you get more useful input?
I would ask follow-up questions like what specifically feels confusing or what could be improved to get more useful feedback.

## How can you ensure that design critique sessions are productive rather than overwhelming?
By keeping feedback respectful, focusing on the main issues first, and avoiding too much criticism all at once.

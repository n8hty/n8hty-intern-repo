# How UX Research Informs Design Decisions

## If research contradicts a designer’s personal preference, what should happen?
If research contradicts a designer’s personal preference, the research should be prioritized because UX design should focus on user needs, not personal taste. 
For example, a designer may prefer a more complex layout, but if users in find it overwhelming, the simpler design would be the better choice.

## How can UX designers ensure that their work aligns with real user needs?
UX designers can align their work with real user needs through usability testing, interviews, surveys, and observing how users interact with the product. 
Regular feedback and testing help confirm whether the design is actually useful and easy to use.

## What risks come with relying on assumptions rather than research when designing an interface?
Relying on assumptions can lead to confusing interfaces, poor usability, and features that users do not actually need. 
In Focus Bear, assuming users want many customization options without testing could make the app feel overwhelming instead of helpful.

# Data Privacy & Confidentiality

## What steps can you take to ensure you handle data securely in your daily tasks?
I can handle data securely by using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, keeping software updated, and only accessing information necessary for 
my work. I will also be careful when opening links or attachments and follow company privacy policies.

## How should you store, share, and dispose of sensitive information safely?
Sensitive information should be stored in approved and secure platforms, shared only with authorized individuals through secure channels, and deleted or 
disposed of properly when it is no longer needed. Physical documents containing confidential information should also be securely destroyed.

## What are some common mistakes that lead to data privacy issues, and how can they be avoided?
Common mistakes include using weak passwords, sending information to the wrong person, storing files in unsecured locations, and leaving devices unlocked. 
These issues can be avoided by using strong passwords, verifying recipients before sharing files, using approved storage systems, and locking devices when not 
in use.

I will make it a habit to verify file permissions and recipients before sharing any information and lock my computer whenever I step away from my workspace.

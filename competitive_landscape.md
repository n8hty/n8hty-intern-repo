# Understand competitive landscape

## What makes Focus Bear different from these apps?
Focus Bear is different from these apps because it combines productivity, habit-building, and focus management all in a single app. 
It attempts to create a complete productivity system which gives a strong emphasis on helping neurodivergent users.

## If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?
I would choose Focus Bear over competitors because it has multiple productivity features that can help its users manage their focus and wellbeing.
I also appreciate that it is designed for neurodivergent users in mind, making it feel more supportive and personalized. Its app blocking feature also 
gives you a moment to remember and change your mind about opening a particular blocked app. I would recommend making it load longer to test the user's patience and
making them think twice if it is worth the wait.

## What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t?
I believe what Focus Bear lacks compared to other apps is a quick access to music, a calendar system, and a simpler onboarding flow. 
Users may want to listen to music without having to enter "Focus session" mode especially since the free version is limited to 25mins. 
A calendar system may also help display tasks and habits in a less overwhelming and more organized way. Additionally, simplifying the onboarding process 
through better visuals and guidance could improve clarity for first-time users and make the app feel approachable despite its large number of features.

## Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?
I believe that although Focus Bear offers many useful features, the large number of options can sometimes make the UI feel overwhelming. 
Being overwhelmed is what causes people to lose interest before even starting, therefore I think that the app would benefit if they balance it out with 
greater simplicity in its design and clearer user experience flow.

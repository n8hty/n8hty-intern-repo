# Iterating Based on User Feedback & Product Constraints

## How do UX designers decide which feedback to act on?
I think UX Designers should act on feedback that is consistently mentioned by multiple users, especially when it affects usability and overall user experience.
They should also consider whether the feedback aligns with the product's goals and intended functionality before deciding which iterations should be implemented.

## What are best practices for prioritizing UX iterations based on feedback?
I believe the best practices for prioritizing UX iterations based on feedback include focusing on issues that affect usability, accessibility, 
and user satisfaction. Designers should be able to balance user needs with business goals and prioritize changes that provide improvement to the 
overall user experience.

## How do product constraints (business goals, development effort) impact iteration decisions?
Product constraints such as business goals and development effort helps teams determine which iterations are realistic and achievable.
Teams must be able to balance user needs according to deadlines and company priorities to ensure practical iterations to the product while continuously 
improving the user experience.

## How can UX designers advocate for usability improvements in a fast-paced product environment?
UX designers can advocate for usability improvements in a fast-paced product environment by clearly demonstrating how usability issues affect both users 
and the overall success of the product. By collaborating closely with developers and other team members, they can communicate the impact of certain issues more
effectively and help the team understand why improving the user experience can also benefit business outcomes.

# Reflection

## If a UX change benefits users but conflicts with business goals, how would you approach the discussion?
If a UX change benefits users but conflicts with business goals, I would first try to understand both perspectives and find a balance 
between the user needs and business objectives rather than focusing on only one side. UX improvement can greatly impact the success of a product by 
increasing user satisfaction and retention, but I would also consider the company's priorities and budget limitations when discussing possible solutions.

## How can designers work with PMs and developers to find compromises between ideal UX and technical feasibility?
Designers work with PMs and developers by communicating openly and understanding the limitations and goals of each team. They should also consider 
technical feasibility, development time, and business priorities while discussing potential solutions.
By collaborating early in the process, the team can discuss alternatives and prioritize the most important user needs while 
keeping the product realistic to build and maintain.

## What are the risks of making too many small UX changes too quickly?
Making too many small UX changes too quickly can make the UI difficult for users to adapt to and can negatively impact the overall user experience.
It may also create unintended usability issues and make it harder for teams to properly evaluate whether each iteration is actually improving the product.
